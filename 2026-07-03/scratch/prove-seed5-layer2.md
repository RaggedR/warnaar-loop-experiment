### Task 3: Structural Analysis of Key Polynomial Indices

**Observation 1: Weight structure of K_u in the decomposition.**

The monomials appearing in the key polynomials of the Q_1 decomposition are NOT at level d. Instead, they are at small levels (1, 2, ...). Specifically:

For d=7, c=(3,2,2), the Q_1 monomials by total degree:
- degree 1: x^{(0,0,1)}, x^{(0,1,0)} (mult 2), x^{(1,0,0)} (mult 2)
- degree 2: x^{(0,0,2)}, x^{(0,1,1)}, x^{(0,2,0)}, x^{(1,0,1)}, x^{(1,1,0)}, x^{(2,0,0)}

The degree-1 and degree-2 monomials are exactly the compositions of 1 and 2 into 3 parts. Total: 3 + 6 = 9 distinct monomials with total multiplicity 11.

**Observation 2: K_{(0,0,m)} is the complete homogeneous symmetric function h_m.**

K_{(0,0,m)}(x1,x2,x3) = sum_{a+b+c=m} x1^a x2^b x3^c = h_m(x1,x2,x3).

This is the character of the m-th symmetric power of the fundamental representation of GL_3. At (q, q^2, q^3), it has dim = (m+1)(m+2)/2.

**Observation 3: Demazure characters vs Schur functions.**

The K_u appearing in the decompositions are NOT Schur functions (irreducible characters) in general. K_{(0,1,0)} has dim 2 and weights {(0,1,0), (1,0,0)}, which is a Demazure truncation of s_{(1,0,0)} (which has dim 3). Similarly, K_{(0,2,0)} has dim 3 and weights {(0,2,0), (1,1,0), (2,0,0)}, a truncation of h_2.

This means Q_1 is NOT the character of a direct sum of irreducible GL_3 representations. It is a sum of Demazure characters -- characters of Demazure modules (submodules of irreducibles generated by extremal weight vectors under Borel subalgebra action).

**Observation 4: The Q_2 decomposition is NON-UNIQUE.**

The LP solver finds different decompositions depending on the objective function. This means the "basis" of key polynomials is overcomplete for expressing Q_2. The decomposition is not canonical without additional structure (e.g., a crystal graph that selects the Demazure components).

**Observation 5: Q_n is NOT Q_1^n.**

Q_2 has max degree 24, while Q_1^2 has max degree 12. The degree grows quadratically: deg(Q_n) ~ 6n^2 for c=(3,2,2). So Q_n cannot be any polynomial function of Q_1. This rules out naive "tensor product" interpretations.

### Task 4: Connection to Affine Schubert Calculus

**The step-size problem (revisited).**

In Layer 1, I noted that Borodin's product formula has q-Pochhammer step t = 3+d, while standard Grothendieck/Schubert factors have step 1. The synthesis suggested affine Schubert calculus could match step t naturally.

In the affine Grassmannian Gr_G for G = SL_3, the equivariant Schubert classes are indexed by elements of the extended affine Weyl group. The Peterson-Lam-Shimozono theorem identifies the Pontryagin homology of Gr_G with a subalgebra of the affine nil-Hecke ring. The Schubert structure constants in this setting involve q-Pochhammer symbols at step equal to the Coxeter number h (or dual Coxeter number h*).

For SL_3: h = h* = 3. For the AFFINE algebra at level d, the relevant step would be... Actually the step t = 3+d appears naturally as the circumference of the cylinder in the cylindric partition model. This is the level-rank duality: cylindric partitions of profile c with k=3 parts and d = sum(c) correspond to level-3 representations of the affine Lie algebra at rank d (or vice versa by level-rank duality).

Under level-rank duality (Nakajima-type), the cylindric partition generating function F_c(z,q) should be related to a character of a level-3 module of hat{sl}_d (or hat{sl}_{d+3} with some twist). The Demazure truncation by max <= n would then correspond to a Demazure module in this affine algebra.

**Concrete proposal:** Q_{n,c}(q) = graded dimension of the intersection of a Demazure module D_n at depth n in the level-3 standard module Lambda of hat{sl}_{d+3} (or appropriate twisted affine algebra) with the weight space determined by the profile c.

This would explain:
- Positivity (graded dim of a vector space)
- The evaluation Q_n(1) = (base)^n (dim of the Demazure module grows exponentially)
- The key polynomial decomposition (restriction of the Demazure crystal to finite type GL_3)
- The profile dependence (different weight spaces)

**What I cannot verify:** Without access to affine crystal computation software (like SageMath's crystal implementation), I cannot directly enumerate the sl_3 affine crystals and check the character match. This is a concrete computational task that should be done with SageMath.

## Approach

### Angle of Attack

The key polynomial decomposition approach proves positivity if we can show that Q_{n,c}(q) ALWAYS decomposes into nonneg integer combinations of K_u(q, q^2, q^3). The computational evidence strongly supports this for d = 2, 4, 5, 7 and n = 0, 1, 2, 3.

### What a Counterexample Looks Like

1. A profile c and n where Q_{n,c}(q) has a negative coefficient (disproves main conjecture)
2. A profile c and n where Q_{n,c}(q) cannot be written as sum a_u K_u(q,q^2,q^3) with a_u >= 0 (disproves key polynomial approach only)

### Confidence

- Conjecture true: 99.5% (stronger evidence now from d=7)
- Key polynomial approach: 65% (up from 40% in Layer 1; the d=7 decomposition working significantly strengthens the case, but the non-uniqueness of Q_2 decomposition and lack of structural understanding remain concerns)

**Key Lemma:** "For each n and valid profile c, there exists a finite union of Demazure crystal components B_1, ..., B_k (in some affine sl_3 crystal at level d) such that Q_{n,c}(q) = sum_i char(B_i)(q, q^2, q^3)."

## Stuck: Connecting Cylindric Partitions to Demazure Crystals

### What I'm trying to show
That the combinatorial objects counted by Q_{n,c}(q) can be organized into a crystal graph whose connected components are Demazure crystals.

### Why I can't show it
1. The objects counted by Q_{n,c}(q) are NOT individual cylindric partitions (the alternating sum removes the direct bijection).
2. The (zq;q)_inf factor creates cancellation, so Q_{n,c}(q) counts some *signed* combination of cylindric partitions, where the miracle is that the signs always cancel.
3. Without knowing what objects are being counted, I can't define crystal operators on them.

### What would unstick me
A manifestly positive combinatorial interpretation of Q_{n,c}(q) -- i.e., a SET of objects (not a signed sum) with a natural q-weighting that sums to Q_{n,c}(q). Once such objects are identified, crystal operators might be definable on them.

Alternatively: a direct identification of Q_{n,c}(q) with a known crystal character formula from the affine Lie algebra literature. This would require matching the Borodin product formula with a character formula for a specific module.

### Key Structural Observations

1. **K_{(0,0,1)} always appears** -- either once or twice. This is the fundamental GL_3 character h_1 = x_1 + x_2 + x_3, contributing dim 3.

2. **Max key level grows with profile asymmetry**: For "balanced" profiles like (3,2,2) and (3,3,2), the max key level is small (2-3). For asymmetric profiles like (5,2,1) and (4,2,1), monomial key polynomials at high levels appear. These are K_u with u dominant (e.g., K_{(3,1,1)} or K_{(3,2,1)}), which are just monomials q^{sum e_i*u_i}.

3. **Profile dependence**: The decomposition depends on the specific profile c, not just on d. This matches the known fact that Q_{1,c}(q) depends on c (not just d), though Q_{1,c}(1) depends only on d.

4. **The "generic" pieces are low-level Demazure characters**: The multi-term key polynomials in the decomposition have small indices (levels 1-3), which are symmetric function building blocks. The profile-specific pieces are high-level dominant keys (monomials).
