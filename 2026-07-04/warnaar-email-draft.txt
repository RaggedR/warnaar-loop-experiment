Subject: Two proved cases of your cylindric fermionic-form conjectures, and a bounded refinement at d=4

Dear Professor Warnaar,

I have been working on the conjectures in your paper on the A2
Andrews-Gordon identities and cylindric partitions -- Conjecture 2.7
(nonnegativity of Q_{n,c}(q)) and the cylindric fermionic-form
Conjectures 2.5 and 2.11 -- and would like to share some results, in
case they are of interest. To be clear at the outset: these are
partial results -- isolated cases -- and the general conjectures
remain wide open. Everything builds directly on published work of
Corteel-Welsh, Kanade-Russell and Uncu; the new content is mainly the
glue, and the key connecting identities have been formally verified in
Lean 4 (details below).

1. The first two cases of the fermionic-form conjectures beyond k=2.

(a) Conjecture 2.5 at k=3: for the balanced profile (3,3,2) at d=8
(modulus 11), G_{(3,3,2)} equals the k=3 fermionic sum, so
Q_{n,(3,3,2)} is a manifestly positive finite sum. The proof is a
short chain of established results: your finite form (the a=-1 case of
Proposition 7.1), a coefficientwise limit, an exact Pochhammer split,
one of the Kanade-Russell contiguous relations, and Uncu's 2024
modulus-11 theorem.

(b) Conjecture 2.11 at k=4: the same template settles the balanced
profile (4,3,3) at d=10 (modulus 13), and the chain is in fact
SHORTER: the a=+1 case of your finite form, in the same limit, after
the same Pochhammer split, lands verbatim on the pair of series in
Uncu's proved modulus-13 expression for H_{(4,3,3)} -- no contiguous
relation is needed. (Structurally: at modulus 3k-1 the finite form
and the Kanade-Russell list differ by one contiguous relation; at
modulus 3k+1 they coincide.) So the template appears to scale to
whichever moduli acquire proved Kanade-Russell expressions.

As far as we know these are the first proved cases of either cylindric
conjecture beyond k=2. In fairness of attribution: Uncu's modulus-11
and modulus-13 theorems are computer-assisted proofs (ideal-membership
certificates in the ancillary files of arXiv:2301.01359), so both
cases inherit that dependency.

2. Bounded analogues at d=4. Since d=4 lies in your proved set, the
positivity itself is not new; what may be of interest is the bounded
refinement. Writing H_{c,m} = (q;q)_m F_{c,m} for the max-part <= m
truncation, we show, for every profile at d=4,

    H_{c,m} = sum_{n <= m} [m,n]_q Q_{n,c},

i.e. the bounded generating functions have a nonnegative q-binomial
expansion whose coefficients are exactly the Q_{n,c}, with explicit
manifestly positive forms for all five orbits (obtained from the
bounded fermionic forms in Corteel-Welsh's "The A2 Rogers-Ramanujan
identities revisited"). Two of the five orbits require what we call
absorption identities to make positivity manifest; these are exact
identities in Z[q] proved by q-Pascal manipulations. Monotonicity
F_{c,m} >= F_{c,m-1} at d=4 follows.

Underlying both is a simple general observation that may already be
known to you: for gcd(d,3)=1, Conjecture 2.7 at a profile c is
equivalent to the statement that the sequence (H_{c,m})_m admits a
coefficientwise-nonnegative q-binomial expansion -- and the expansion
coefficients are then necessarily Q_{n,c}. So the bounded and
unbounded positivity questions coincide exactly.

The connecting arguments (the q-binomial inversion, the absorption
identities, and the assembly of the modulus-11 and d=4 proofs from
their literature inputs as named hypotheses) are formalized sorry-free
in Lean 4 over Mathlib; since Mathlib currently has no Gaussian
binomials, this included building a small reusable q-binomial library.
The modulus-13 chain has not yet been formalized (its hypotheses would
mirror the modulus-11 ones). The code is at
https://github.com/RaggedR/warnaar-glue (the attached note includes a
table mapping each statement to its Lean theorem). Separately, we
have verified Conjecture 2.7 exactly (in Z[q], no truncation) for
d up to 31 in various ranges of n, with no counterexample and no
shrinking of margins.

I attach short writeups. I would be very glad to know whether either
case is already known to you, whether the results are of interest, and
whether you see obstructions to pushing the template to the
non-balanced orbits.
